# Analytical Framework: Casualty-Based Proxy for Mobilization Analysis

## Core method

This study uses **casualty analysis** as a proxy for mapping tactical initiative and force mobilization under conditions of extreme uncertainty. The logic: who died where, and how they arrived, reveals the actual pattern of response when official records of deployment are incomplete or unavailable.

## Theoretical grounding

- **High-reliability organizing (HRO)**: Weick & Sutcliffe — mindful organizing under pressure
- **Latent organizing**: Majchrzak et al. — dormant structures activated in crisis
- **Organizational improvisation and bricolage**: Ciborra; Bechky & Okhuysen — creative recombination under constraint
- **Crisis governance**: Boin, Hart, Stern & Sundelius — leadership and decision-making during transboundary crises

## Key concept: Embedded Response Capacity (ERC)

The set of structural, cultural, and operational capabilities that enable frontline units to mobilize, coordinate, and act effectively when formal C2 systems are degraded or disabled.

ERC components (tested via H1–H5):
1. **Temporal two-wave structure** (H1): stationed first, self-joined second
2. **Voluntary mobilization** (H2): significant self-joining without orders
3. **ERC profile predictors** (H3): SOF, reserves, officer status
4. **Ad hoc team formation** (H4): multi-branch convergence at attack sites
5. **Operational stabilization** (H5): response presence reduces kidnapping outcomes

## Datasets and confidence

- Dataset 1: N=37 locations → H4, H5
- Dataset 2: N=369 fatalities → H1, H2, H3
- Confidence tiers: VERIFIED / LIKELY / UNCONFIRMED (see `data/metadata.json`)

## Statistical methods

- Mann-Whitney U, Welch's t-test, KS test (H1)
- Binomial tests, Chi-square by subgroup (H2)
- Logistic regression, McFadden R² (H3)
- Spatial diversity metrics (H4)
- Comparative outcome analysis (H5)

## Transliteration

Hebrew terms use conventional English spellings for military units and place names. No formal transliteration system applied (this is not an area studies submission).
